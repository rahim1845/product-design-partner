01 / Why

Three failure modes kill
most products. This skill
exists to prevent them.

Failure — A

Building something nobody wants

A solution in search of a problem. The skill forces the worth-it question first: assumptions ranked by risk, a segment chosen, kill criteria written in advance — before a single screen is designed.

Failure — B

Building something people want, in a way that fails them

A real job, done badly. It holds every flow to named heuristics and evidence: must-haves intact, friction lowest at moments of highest intent, accessibility designed in — not bolted on.

Failure — C

Building something people like that no business can sustain

Loved and unviable. Every recommendation ties the user outcome to a business outcome — retention before growth, must-haves before monetization — and names what each choice costs.

02 / How it works

Diagnose first.
Then apply craft.

The skill never dumps a process on you. It figures out where you actually are, loads only the expertise that phase needs, and leaves you with an artifact you can use.

01

You bring the real situation

"I have an app idea." "Users aren't coming back." "Review this onboarding flow." Any phase, any fidelity — from a napkin sketch to a live product with churn data.

02

It diagnoses your phase

Nine lifecycle phases, from 0 · Worth-it to 8 · Grow. Two or three high-signal questions — who is the user, what outcome matters, what's the riskiest assumption — then it commits.

03

It loads only the mode you need

Ten expert modes route to a nine-file reference library — strategy, research, design craft, validation math, shipping, growth, narrative, AI ethics. No 50-page dumps; just the depth the moment demands.

04

You leave with an artifact

A ranked assumption map. An interview guide that passes Mom Test rules. A usability test plan with real sample-size math. A positioning statement. Every framework named produces something concrete.

04 / Modes

Ten experts.
One partner.

The skill identifies which hat the moment needs and loads only that expertise. Modes stack when the work demands it.

Mode 01

Worth-it

"Should I build this?" — DVF assumption ranking, segment scoring, pre-committed kill criteria.

Mode 02

Discovery partner

Interviews under Mom Test rules, contextual inquiry, synthesis, evidence-backed personas and journeys.

Mode 03

Design partner

Flows, information architecture, interaction, WCAG 2.2 accessibility, internationalization.

Mode 04

Validator

Usability tests, smoke tests, fake doors, A/B with honest sample-size math, PMF measurement.

Mode 05

Critic

Design and strategy review against named heuristics — Nielsen's 10, Fitts, Hick, Jakob — with severity scores.

Mode 06

Shipper & measurer

Design QA, rollout plans, analytics before launch, rollback triggers, post-launch retention reads.

Mode 07

Growth & positioning

Dunford positioning, growth loops over funnels, north star metrics, pricing direction.

Mode 08

Storyteller

Value props, launch copy, naming, decks — selling the transformation, not the feature list.

Mode 09

AI product

UX for anything that generates, predicts, or decides — confidence, failure states, trust, ethics.

Mode 10

Portfolio

Case studies and design storytelling that show judgment, not just deliverables.

06 / Principles

How it operates.

01
Diagnose phase first.Match guidance to where you actually are. Never dump the whole process.
02
Apply frameworks, don't name-drop them.Every framework mentioned must produce a concrete artifact in the reply.
03
Tie user outcomes to business outcomes.And call it out when one is being optimized at the other's expense.
04
Challenge weak thinking, kindly but directly.No flattering bad ideas. No validating assumptions presented as facts.
05
Evidence over opinion.Interviews and observed behavior beat proxies; proxies beat nothing. Research theater is not research.
06
Real sources only.Never fabricate citations, stats, or quotes. Numbers are sourced, or labeled illustrative.
07
Scale to your constraints.A solo founder with one evening gets different methods than a team with research ops.
07 / Install

Two minutes
to a better partner.

The skill is a folder of Markdown — no build step, no dependencies. Clone the repository and copy the skill/ folder into your Claude skills directory.

Then just talk to Claude about your product. The skill triggers on phrases like "validate my idea", "is this worth building", "usability test", "review this flow" — or invoke it directly.

Works in Claude Code, the Claude desktop app, and anywhere Agent Skills are supported.

terminal
# 1 — get the skill
git clone https://github.com/rahim1845/product-design-partner.git

# 2 — install it for Claude
cp -r product-design-partner/skill \
      ~/.claude/skills/product-design-partner
claude
